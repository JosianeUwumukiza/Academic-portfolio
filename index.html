Josiane Uwumukiza

Computer Vision & Machine Learning for Perception and 3D Reconstruction

I am a Computer Science student at Wellesley College (BA expected May 2026) and an undergraduate researcher at MIT's Astrodynamics, Space Robotics, and Controls Lab (ARCLab). My research interests lie at the intersection of 3D computer vision, representation learning, and autonomous systems.

My current undergraduate thesis focuses on using 3D reconstructed spacecraft objects in their pose estimation. I work with DINOv3 for 2D features, DGCNN for 3D encoding, and Hunyuan3D-2 for reconstruction. I am also collaborating with the Conformable Decoders group at MIT Media Lab on AI-driven ultrasound image analysis and its integration with wearable systems.
